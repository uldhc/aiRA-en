About aiRA/h1>

The aiRA tool was created by the Centre for Digital Humanities at the University of Latvia as part of the ȬPEN project (No. ZDA-LIP 2025/2). The project promotes access to knowledge and public participation in the creation of knowledge. This tool is an open-source project and is available on GitHub.

It was developed by adapting the aBBi tool created by the Society of Swedish Literature in Finland (Svenska litteratursällskapet i Finland – SLS).

aiRA palīdz ģenerēt attēlu alternatīvos tekstus (alt text) un transkripcijas, izmantojot mākslīgo intelektu (AI). Tāpat rīks spēj kodēt transkripcijas TEI XML formātā, kas paredzēts arhivēšanas vai pētniecības vajadzībāmaiRA helps generate alternative text descriptions, or alt text, for images, as well as transcriptions, using artificial intelligence (AI). The tool can also encode transcriptions in TEI XML format, which is intended for archiving or research purposes.

You use your own personal OpenAI or Google Gemini API key, and the tool runs entirely in your browser – no backend server is involved. Images from your browser are sent directly to the selected AI service provider, the result is received, and that is all.

This means:

  • You control the costs. OpenAI or Google bills you directly based on your usage and their standard API rates.
  • You control the data. Nothing goes through our servers. We do not see your images, your text, or your API key.
  • You choose the service provider. Choose the AI service you trust and for which you have an available key.

Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly does aiRA do?

You upload or select an image, and aiRA sends it to an AI model, either OpenAI or Google depending on your configuration, together with instructions: either to describe the image for alternative text, or to transcribe the text visible in it. If you are performing a transcription, you can set the result to be output in TEI XML format.

Do I need an account to use aiRA?

You do not need an account in our system, but you do need an OpenAI or Google API key, because aiRA uses these companies’ AI models to carry out the actual image analysis.

How can I get an API key?

For OpenAI:

  1. Go toplatform.openai.com and register or sign in.
  2. and register or sign in.
  3. Create a new secret key and copy it somewhere safe – you will not be able to view it again.
  4. Add payment information, because API usage is a paid service. This is separate from a ChatGPT Plus subscription.
  5. Paste the key into the aiRA settings in step 2.

For Google Gemini:

  1. Go to Google AI Studio at aistudio.google.com.
  2. Sign in with your Google account.
  3. Click “Get API key” and create one.
  4. Paste the key into the aiRA settings.

Is the tool free to use?

aiRA itself is free. However, your API key is used to generate descriptions and transcriptions, and AI service providers charge for this usage. Usually, the cost per image is a few cents or less than one cent, but if you work with large volumes, the costs add up. Before intensive use, please check the current OpenAI or Google pricing.

What happens to my images and API key? Is anything saved?

Nothing is saved or sent to us. aiRA runs entirely in your browser:

  • Your API key is stored only in your browser locally, so that you do not have to enter it again each time. It is never transmitted to us;
  • Your images are sent directly from your browser to the OpenAI or Google API – they never reach our servers;
  • We do not perform any logging, we do not collect analytics about your images, and we do not save copies of your results. We do not see them.

In short: your data relationship is with OpenAI or Google, not with us. Please refer to their respective privacy policies to learn how they process data sent to their APIs, for example whether data is used for model training. This differs depending on the service provider and account type, so please check their current terms.

Can I use this tool with sensitive or private images?

Be careful. Although aiRA itself does not save anything, your images still leave your device and are sent to a third-party AI service provider. If you are working with sensitive, personal, or restricted-access materials, review OpenAI’s and Google’s data usage and retention policies, and follow your organization’s data processing guidelines.

Which AI models can I use?

Several OpenAI or Google models that have “vision,” or image-understanding, capabilities. The exact list is configurable and may be updated over time – check the settings panel for the available options.

Can I customize how the descriptions are written?

Yes. It is possible to adjust the AI’s reasoning level and temperature, or creativity, which affects the language of the descriptions. You can also edit the descriptions after they are generated and translate them into other languages.

In which languages can text be transcribed?

aiRA can transcribe texts that use the Latin alphabet, including letters with diacritical marks, such as “ā”, “ģ”, and “ø”.

Does aiRA work on mobile devices?

Yes, it is a responsive web application. However, for uploading and reviewing larger volumes of images, it will be more convenient to use a larger screen.
